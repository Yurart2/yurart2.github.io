Pencil L. Hunter with His Missing Hat 24" x 40"
First Prize - Honorable Mention - Third Prize
Artist's Collection

 Upon answering patrons questions about the title of this painting, Donn has mentioned two things. First, the idea for the painting was generated from reading Oliver Sacks' book ,The Man who mistook his wife for a hat. And secondly it reflects a little truth about all of us by being “A study peculiar to speculation about the habit of misplacing things”. This painting has been juried into several very competitive national art exhibitions. Learn more about this painting by visiting the Awards Gallery.



Blade Cavanaugh 5 A.M.
Oil on Canvas
24" x 40"
Collection of Glenn and Carol Dagleish


Blade had an early start to get to the county courthouse to do his civic duty: Jury Duty. We can not predict if he will be selected for a jury or not. But making himself available after he received notice to report is one important responsibility of being an American citizen.
   



America, God shed his grace on thee
16” x 24” oil on canvas

This painting was juried into two 2011 New York City national art exhibitions: first by Audubon Artists, Inc. for their 69th Annual Exhibition in September, followed by Allied Artists of America, Inc., for their 98th Annual Exhibition in November.  This was quite an honor.  Upon attending the Allied Artists' reception, I viewed 164 paintings from artists in 29 states and sculptures from 17 states, all showing a full range of subjects and prices.
